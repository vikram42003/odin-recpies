Fish in Foil

Baked fish in foil is a "no-smell" fish recipe, and the only one I make for my family (I hate fish, they love it!). Take foil packets to the table for service, and keep the packets around for wrapping and discarding bones and skin. Then when dinner's done, haul those babies to the outside trash.
~ Denyse (original author)

Here is a link to the original recipe by Denyse.

Prep Time: 10mins Cook Time: 15mins Total Time: 25mins Servings: 2

Nutritional Info (per serving)

Calories: 213 Fat: 11g  Carbs: 8g  Protein: 24g

Ingredients

  • 2 rainbow trout fillets
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 teaspoon garlic salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 sheets heavy-duty aluminium foil
  • 1 fresh jalapeno pepper, sliced
  • 1 lemon, sliced

Steps to make the best Fish in Foil

Step 1

Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).

Step 2

Rinse and pat fillets dry. Rub fillets with olive oil, and season with garlic salt and black pepper. Place each fillet on a large sheet of aluminum foil. Top with jalapeno slices, and squeeze the juice from the ends of the lemon over fillets. Arrange remaining lemon slices on top of fillets.

Step 3

Carefully seal all edges of the foil to form enclosed packets. Place packets on a baking sheet.

Step 4

Bake in the preheated oven until fish flakes easily with a fork, 15 to 20 minutes depending on size of fillets.

Now, All done, Enjoy your Fish in Foil.
